Problem

Designers, especially newbie design learners, don't have a channel to bond with relatively experienced designers.

This project is based on one of my previous research and design -- Design Mentorship App. Hence, this app has my user research and design as a basis.

Target Users

Mentee

Anja | 26 years old | UX Student

UX Design learners who don’t have design background but want to switch into UX.

She wants to seek career advice or portfolio feedback from experienced designers.

Based on my research at Design Mentorship App

Mentor

Emma | 36 years old | Design Manager

An experienced designer and manager who wants to contribute back to the design community.

She wants to help sprouting designers and share her experiences.

Takeaway

A new mindset to solve problems.

Habits to solve problems

Debugging for me is painstaking while satisfying because every bug solved means I learn to solve a new problem. In the process of debugging, I gained new habits to solve problems -- I will break big tasks into smaller steps and debug piece by piece because each small win is motivating and it's also easier to hone in on the buggy line of code.

Patience & calm

Throughout the process of debugging and learning programming logic, I gradually grew more patience and calm in the face of difficult issues , which I lacked previously.

Confidence to solve technical problems

I gained more confidence and techniques in understanding technical problems. I have always regarded myself not material for coding, but this project not only boosted my coding skills but also increased my confidence in solving technical problems.
