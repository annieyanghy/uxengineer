Problem

AI is already hard to understand for the majority of people, and the process of building a model with the old version of Decanter was lengthy and hard to navigate.

My Goal

As the UX designer at MoBagel Inc., my major workload is to redesign the user interface for Decanter AI, the main product that empowers data analysts with rapidly built Auto Machine Learning (AutoML) models.

AutoML, AI and Data Science are hard-core subjects, and my duty is to transform the technology into a user-friendly product that can be easily used. It took me lots of efforts the beginning to first roughly understand what AutoML is. The speciality of designing an enterprise-faced product is that it focuses so much on the logic and the technology itself, instead of fancy and market-driven visual imagery.

Design Process


I joined the product development process right before the phase of HI-fi UI and continued through the process to the alpha release. Before I joined, the Product Managers had had a blueprint for the totally renovating product release and I was referred to the company to help realize the UX renovation of the new release.

What had been done before I joined:
  • User feedback interview: the product team had done interviews with some of our clients (real users) before I joined the company.
  • Brainstorming: based on the interviews with clients, the brainstorming sessions and ideas had been created before I joined
  • Wireframes: wireframes for the product had been roughly sketched out
    •
    What was my mission:
  • To improve the user flow.
  • To flesh out the wireframe into hi-fi mockup.
  • To perfect the documentation of the design handoff.
  • To do testing on the mockup and collected user feedback by interviews.

    Takeaway

    Since this is the first time as a UX Designer, I learned tons in this project. Here I listed 3 main things I improved, which are mostly about the mindset.

    Confidence

    At the beginning of the career transition, I was not confident in every decision I made and may sometimes doubt my own ability on UX design â€” Yes, the Imposter Syndrome (take a looks at how Times Magazine talked about it.) How I gained confidence? Acknowledge my inadequacy and realize that everyone is learning.


    Never hesitate to ask WHY

    In the beginning, as I joined this AI startup, I was so intimidated by all of my colleagues and felt out of place because I seem to be the only one with a background in humanities and arts and no nothing about machine learning, data processing, and AI. So, I was very hesitant to ask Product Manager the reason for features he planed in the product. How did I start to ask why, proactively? As I gained more confidence in my work, I started to realize: asking the why is my job because I want to find out what users want!
